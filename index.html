Three Scenarios to 2030

Regulated Regionalism

  • Policy fragmentation → intra‑regional mobility
  • Rise of emerging regional destinations
  • Need for agile compliance & diversified recruitment

Hybrid Multiversity

  • Blended/hybrid models restructure where + how students learn
  • TNE & campus networks expand; modular, portable learning
  • Digital/AI maturity becomes a strategic differentiator

Talent Race Rebound

  • Global competition for students as future citizens/workforce
  • Visa & work‑rights policy becomes decisive
  • Employability metrics drive student choice
Source: QS Global Student Flows 2025

Destination Trends

  • US remains #1 but growth slows without policy shifts; top‑4 share dips toward ~35% by 2030
  • UK projected to approach ~900k by 2030 under favorable policy
  • Middle East & Asia hubs (UAE, KSA, HK, SK, Japan) strengthen as regional magnets
  • Top 30 destinations host ~90% of flows
Implication: compete where we’re strong; partner where hubs are rising.
Source: QS Global Student Flows 2025

Origin & Flow Shifts

Rising Outbound

  • India: sustained outbound demand, especially STEM/postgrad
  • Vietnam & Indonesia: affordability + visa openness → Japan/Europe growth
  • Nigeria: youth bulge supports demand; volatility by policy/economy
  • Cameroon & Morocco: stronger flows to UK/France

Intra‑regional Tilt

  • China → Japan/South Korea share grows under regionalism
  • Gulf & East Asia destinations expand English‑medium capacity
  • Scenario‑dependent rebalancing among Big Four vs regional hubs
Source: QS Global Student Flows 2025
